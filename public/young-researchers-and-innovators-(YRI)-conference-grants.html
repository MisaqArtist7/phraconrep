Young Researchers and Innovators (YRI) Conference Grants

Are you a young researcher or innovator under the age of 40? If so, you may be eligible for a YRI Conference Grant offered by COST Action Phraconrep (CA22115).

These grants are designed to support the participation of young researchers and innovators in high-level international conferences that are not organized by the COST Action. The goal is to foster professional growth, visibility, and collaboration opportunities within the research community.

Eligibility

  • Open to young researchers and innovators under 40 years of age.
  • Applicants must be affiliated with institutions in COST Full/Cooperating Member Countries, Near Neighbor Countries (NNCs), or European Research and Technology Development (RTD) organizations.
  • An accepted oral or poster presentation relevant to the scope of the Action is required.

Objectives

These grants aim to:

  • Strengthen professional networks,
  • Increase visibility in the research community,
  • Support knowledge exchange through international conference participation.

How to Apply

Applications must be submitted via the eCOST System (https://e-services.cost.eu/). If you do not have an eCOST account, you can create one through the system. Please note: The YRI conference should end before October 2025. If you would like to attend a conference in October 2025, please contact the Grant Awarding Coordinator first.

Required Documents

YRI Conference Grant application form (template available on eCOST), including:

  • A description of the relevance of the conference to the Action,
  • A motivation statement outlining the potential impact on your career,
  • Curriculum Vitae (including a list of academic publications),
  • Brief description of your involvement in COST Action Phraconrep,
  • Letter of acceptance from the conference organizers.
  • Copy of the abstract or poster submitted to the conference.
  • Estimated budget listing expected expenses.

Note: In the case of joint presentations, only one presenter may be awarded the grant.

Post-Conference Reporting

  • A report to the Action Management Committee detailing the presentation’s outcomes, including increased visibility and new contacts for potential collaboration.
  • Certificate of attendance.
  • Conference program or book of abstracts/proceedings indicating the grantee’s presentation.
  • A copy of the presented oral or poster contribution.
